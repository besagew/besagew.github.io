This is a bunch of music I like. All album covers here are edited to be 8-16 colors for a bit more cohesion. Also cuz i think it's cool.

The "Favorites" category may not always be my actual favorite songs since those can change frequently, it's usually 2-3 songs I think represent the album best.

Title Artist Released Rating Comment Favorites
Mista Thug Isolation Lil Ugly Mane 02/11/2012 5 One of the best hip hop records of all time in terms of nailing a sound, iterating on it, and sticking with it throughout the runtime. Back to back I don't think there's a single song in MTI I would skip, every single one is crazy-ass jazz and memphis samples, twisted beats, and consistently really clever and catchy wordplay from the Mane. It's frankly a little unbelievable that nearly this entire project is made by one dude; Sonically it stands alone over 10 years after it's release despite many attempting to emulate the horrorcore memphis rap style. Ugly did it best here in 2012. Serious Shit, Wishmaster, Cup Fulla Beetlejuice
Exmilitary Death Grips 04/25/2011 5 Yeah this is a given. One of the most creative and exhilarating albums I've ever heard and my introduction to music as a whole. Front to back every song is a stand out; DG's production is loud and primal on tracks like Blood Creepin and Takyon, electronic and glitchy on tracks like Culture Shock and Known For It, and somehow flows perfectly with Ride's manic delivery and lyricism, particularly on Beware which is likely the best album opener of all time. Exmilitary will continue to sound like an album from 40 years into the future regardless of when you listen. Beware, Takyon
None Shall Pass Aesop Rock 08/28/2007 3.7 One of my favorites from Aes, I think he really found his style with this release. Even the tracks I don't love manage to paint a really unique and grimey picture sonically, and Aesop delivers some of his best singles ever on this album. Great storytelling, societal commentary, Aesop's thesaurus rap, and jazzy instrumentals from Blockhead and El-P make this a solid album. Coffee, None Shall Pass, The Harbor is Yours
Hiding Places Billy Woods 03/29/2019 4.7 Never has an album sounded more like the cover. Hiding Places is dismal, bleak, and depressing. Billy Wood's half spoken-word half shouting flow (That I admittedly get sick of after a while) backed up by Kenny Segal's atmospheric and haunting instrumentals give this album a uniquly nihilistic sound. I could see myself laying in bed and rotting away to this album, easily one of my favorites from Woods. Spider Hole, Steak Knives, Red Dust
