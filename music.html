This is a bunch of music I like. All album covers here are edited to be 8-16 colors for a bit more cohesion. Also cuz i think it's cool. Albums are rated on a 0-5 scale, with a 5 being an album I believe to be both nearly perfect and innovative in the genre, and 0 being actively unpleasant to listen to.

The "Favorites" category may not always be my actual favorite songs since those can change frequently, it's usually 2-3 songs I think represent the album best.

Title Artist Released Rating Comment Favorites
Mista Thug Isolation Lil Ugly Mane 02/11/2012 5.0 One of the best hip hop records of all time in terms of nailing a sound, iterating on it, and sticking with it throughout the runtime. Back to back I don't think there's a single song in MTI I would skip, every single one is crazy-ass jazz and memphis samples, twisted beats, and consistently really clever and catchy wordplay from the Mane. It's frankly a little unbelievable that nearly this entire project is made by one dude; Sonically it stands alone over 10 years after it's release despite many attempting to emulate the horrorcore memphis rap style. Ugly did it best here in 2012. Serious Shit, Wishmaster, Cup Fulla Beetlejuice
Exmilitary Death Grips 04/25/2011 5.0 Yeah this is a given. One of the most creative and exhilarating albums I've ever heard and my introduction to music as a whole. Front to back every song is a stand out; DG's production is militant and oppresive glitch hip-hop fronted by some of the most unique and abstract lyrics and delivery ever. Beware is likely the best album opener of all time. Exmilitary will continue to sound like an album from 40 years into the future regardless of when you listen, decidedly one of the best albums of all time. Beware, Takyon, Culture Shock
None Shall Pass Aesop Rock 08/28/2007 3.6 One of my favorites from Aes, I think he really found his style with this release. Even the tracks I don't love manage to paint a really unique and grimey picture sonically, and Aesop delivers some of his best singles ever on this album. Great storytelling, societal commentary, Aesop's thesaurus rap, and jazzy instrumentals from Blockhead and El-P make this a really solid album. Coffee, None Shall Pass, The Harbor is Yours
Hiding Places Billy Woods 03/29/2019 4.5 Never has an album sounded more like the cover. Billy Wood's half spoken-word half shouting flow (That I admittedly get sick of after a while) backed up by Kenny Segal's atmospheric and haunting instrumentals give this album a uniquly nihilistic sound. Not angry nihilism like Death Grips, but miserable and depressing nihilism. I could see myself laying in bed and rotting away to this, easily one of my favorite hip-hop albums. Spider Hole, Steak Knives, Red Dust
Three Sided Tape Volume One Lil Ugly Mane 08/7/2013 4.2 Despite being basically a demo tape for Lil Ugly Mane, Volume One of the three sided tape is a consistently interesting and exciting array of songs. You'd think an album with this much genre bending would sound less coherent. deer gas, can sex me, ___________, forever i b stangin
Three Sided Tape Volume Two Lil Ugly Mane 08/11/2013 3.1 While still a sonically great set of tracks, Volume 2 is noticably weaker than the other two volumes. The highlight tracks are some of the best in the entire project, but they're surrounded by skits, interludes, and some less interesting tracks. Amazing by most artist standards, just middling for Ugly Mane's. Floss Thru Ya Town, Go Away, Misery
